Dr. Jonas Salk
The father of the polio vaccine
The work of Dr. Salk is in the highest tradition of selfless and dedicated medical research. He has provided a means for the control of a dread disease. By helping scientists in other countries with technical information; by offering to them the strains of seed virus and professional aid so that the production of vaccine can be started by them everywhere; by welcoming them to his laboratory that they may gain a fuller knowledge, Dr. Salk is a benefactor of mankind. - Dwight Eisenhower
Timeline:
October 28, 1914- Born in New York
1948- Begins efforts against polio.
1954- Field tests of polio vaccine begin.
1955- Polio vaccine declared a success.
19995- Passes away.
